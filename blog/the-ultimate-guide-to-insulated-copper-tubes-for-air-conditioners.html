The Ultimate Guide to Insulated Copper Tubes for Air Conditioners

BENRO INDUSTRIES·March 16, 2025

Introduction

In the world of air conditioning and refrigeration systems, the quality of components significantly affects system performance. One of the most essential components is the insulated copper tube, which helps maintain efficiency, minimise heat loss and extend the lifespan of HVAC systems.

This guide covers everything you need to know about insulated copper tubes, including their benefits, applications and industry insights backed by statistics and calculations.

What Are Insulated Copper Tubes?

Insulated copper tubes are copper pipes covered with polyethylene (PE) foam insulation. The insulation layer prevents heat transfer and condensation, making them essential for air conditioning, refrigeration and HVAC systems.

For high-quality options, explore our Twin and Single Insulated Copper Tubes.

Key Benefits of Insulated Copper Tubes

1. Improved Energy Efficiency

Insulated copper tubes reduce heat loss and maintain refrigerant temperature, improving the energy efficiency of HVAC systems. According to the U.S. Department of Energy, proper insulation can reduce energy loss by up to 10%.

2. Prevention of Condensation

Temperature differences between the refrigerant and ambient air can cause condensation, leading to corrosion and system failures. Insulated copper tubes prevent moisture build-up, ensuring system longevity.

3. Extended Equipment Lifespan

By reducing corrosion and maintaining temperature stability, insulated copper tubes increase the lifespan of HVAC units, lowering maintenance and replacement costs.

4. High-Temperature Resistance

Copper tubes offer superior heat resistance compared to aluminium and other materials, making them ideal for extreme climate conditions.

BTU Calculation for HVAC Sizing

When selecting the right insulated copper tubes for air conditioners, it’s essential to calculate the British Thermal Unit (BTU) capacity. BTU measures how much heat an air conditioner can remove from a space per hour.

Below is a BTU calculation table based on room size:

Room Size (m²)Recommended BTU CapacitySuitable Air Conditioner Type
9 – 14 m²5,000 – 7,000 BTUWindow AC or Small Split Unit
15 – 23 m²8,000 – 10,000 BTUStandard Split Unit
24 – 35 m²12,000 – 15,000 BTULarge Split or Small Ducted System
36 – 50 m²18,000 – 24,000 BTUCentral Air or Multi-Split System
51 – 70 m²30,000 – 36,000 BTULarge Central Air System

Additional BTU Considerations

  • Add +10% BTU for kitchens due to extra heat from appliances.
  • Increase BTU by +20% for rooms with large windows or high sun exposure.
  • Consider ceiling height — for rooms higher than 3 m, increase BTU by 15%.

Industry Statistics: Copper vs. Aluminium

Many HVAC professionals debate Copper vs. Aluminium Tubes for air conditioning. Here’s how they compare:

FeatureCopper TubesAluminium Tubes
Heat ConductivityHigher (401 W/m·K)Lower (205 W/m·K)
DurabilityMore durableProne to corrosion
FlexibilityLess flexibleMore flexible
CostHigherLower
RepairabilityEasy to repairHarder to weld

Conclusion: copper tubes provide better heat conductivity, durability and repairability, making them the superior choice for HVAC systems. However, aluminium tubes are an affordable alternative for budget-conscious projects.
