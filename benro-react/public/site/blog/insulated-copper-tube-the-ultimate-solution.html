Insulated Copper Tube: The Ultimate Solution for Efficient HVAC Systems

BENRO INDUSTRIES·March 12, 2025

Why Choose Insulated Copper Tubes for HVAC Applications?

Insulated copper tubes are the backbone of modern HVAC systems, ensuring efficient heat transfer, reduced energy loss and enhanced durability. These tubes come with a protective layer that minimises condensation, prevents corrosion and improves overall system efficiency.

Key Benefits of Insulated Copper Tubes

1. Superior Thermal Efficiency

Copper is one of the best conductors of heat, making it the preferred material for air conditioning and refrigeration. When combined with high-quality polyethylene (PE) insulation, these tubes offer maximum thermal efficiency.

2. Prevents Condensation and Corrosion

Uninsulated copper tubes are prone to moisture build-up, leading to corrosion and inefficiency. The insulation layer acts as a barrier against humidity, preventing water droplets from forming on the surface.

3. Increases Energy Savings

By reducing heat loss and energy consumption, insulated copper tubes help lower electricity bills. This makes them ideal for industrial, commercial and residential HVAC systems.

4. Longer Lifespan and Durability

Copper itself is naturally resistant to oxidation and degradation. With added insulation, the tubes are protected from external damage, extending their service life significantly.

Applications of Insulated Copper Tubes

1. Air-Conditioning Systems

Used for refrigerant transportation, insulated copper tubes maintain stable temperature flow without loss of efficiency.

2. Refrigeration Units

Commercial refrigerators and freezers rely on these tubes to ensure proper cooling performance and energy efficiency.

3. Heating and Cooling Networks

In large-scale buildings, HVAC distribution systems utilise insulated copper tubing to maintain consistent temperature regulation.

4. Industrial and Medical Applications

Industries and medical facilities use insulated copper pipes for gas distribution and heat exchange processes.

Copper vs. Aluminium Tubing: Which One is Better?

  • Higher Heat Conductivity — copper transfers heat better than aluminium, ensuring improved efficiency.
  • Easier to Repair — unlike aluminium, which is harder to weld, copper tubing can be easily repaired and soldered.
  • More Durable — copper tubes last longer and withstand higher pressures without cracking.

Standards and Quality Assurance: EN12735 vs. ASTM

  • EN 12735 (European Standard) — designed specifically for refrigeration and air-conditioning applications, ensuring strength and corrosion resistance.
  • ASTM (American Standard) — covers a wider range of materials, often used in North America for various piping systems.

How to Choose the Right Insulated Copper Tube?

  1. Check the Insulation Material — ensure it offers thermal resistance and prevents condensation.
  2. Confirm Compliance with HVAC Standards — always choose EN12735- or ASTM-certified tubes.
  3. Consider the Tube Size and Thickness — select based on your system’s pressure and refrigerant type.
  4. Look for Reliable Manufacturers — opt for trusted brands that offer high-quality and durable products.

Conclusion

Investing in insulated copper tubes ensures maximum efficiency, energy savings and long-term reliability in HVAC systems. Whether for residential, commercial or industrial use, these tubes provide superior performance and durability.
