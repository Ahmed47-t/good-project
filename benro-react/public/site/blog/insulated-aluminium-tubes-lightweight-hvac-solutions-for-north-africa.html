Insulated Aluminium Tubes: Lightweight HVAC Solutions for North Africa

BENRO INDUSTRIES·February 25, 2025

What Are Insulated Aluminium Tubes?

Insulated aluminium tubes are lightweight, corrosion-resistant pipes used in HVAC and refrigeration systems. They consist of seamless aluminium tubing wrapped in polyethylene (PE) foam or rubber insulation to prevent heat transfer and condensation.

Why Aluminium?

  • Lightweight — 60% lighter than copper, reducing installation labour and costs.
  • Cost-Effective — 30–50% cheaper than copper tubes.
  • Corrosion Resistance — natural oxide layer protects against humidity and salt (ideal for coastal Libya).

Key Components

  1. Aluminium Core — 1070 or 6063 alloy (ASTM B209 / EN 573).
  2. Insulation Layer — PE foam (thermal conductivity: 0.0402 W/m·K) or rubber.
  3. UV-Resistant Jacket — protects against North Africa’s harsh sunlight.

Applications of Insulated Aluminium Tubes

1. Residential Air Conditioning

  • Split AC Systems — pre-insulated tubes for connecting indoor/outdoor units.
  • Heat Pumps — lightweight design simplifies rooftop installations.

2. Industrial & Commercial Projects

  • Cold Storage — insulated tubes for غرف تبريد (cold rooms) in food processing.
  • Solar HVAC — compatible with renewable-energy systems.

3. Automotive & Aerospace

  • Cooling Lines — lightweight tubes for electric-vehicle battery cooling.

5 Benefits of Insulated Aluminium Tubes

  1. Energy Efficiency — PE foam reduces heat gain by 40% (ASHRAE Study).
  2. Easy Installation — flexible coils cut labour time by 30%.
  3. Eco-Friendly — recyclable materials align with Algeria’s sustainability goals.
  4. Low Maintenance — resists corrosion and UV damage.
  5. Cost Savings — affordable alternative to copper without sacrificing performance.

Step 2: Select Insulation Type

  • PE Foam — ideal for indoor HVAC (R-value: 3.3).
  • Rubber — better flexibility for tight spaces.

Step 3: Verify Certifications

  • Look for EN 12735-1 or ASTM B209 compliance.
  • Ensure fire ratings (e.g. ASTM E84 Class 1).

Installation Best Practices

  1. Avoid Bends — use tube benders to prevent kinks.
  2. Seal Joints — apply UV-resistant tape at connections.
  3. Test for Leaks — pressure-test with nitrogen before charging refrigerant.

Q: How does aluminium compare to copper?

A: Aluminium is lighter and cheaper but less durable under high pressure. Ideal for residential HVAC.
